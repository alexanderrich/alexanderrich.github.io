I am a doctoral student at the NYU Computation & Cognition Lab, working with Prof. Todd Gureckis. I'm interested in how people learn and make decisions in complex, uncertain environments. My recent work leverages computational models of reinforcement learning and categorization to understand how people can stumble into "learning traps" in which the interplay of learning and decision-making produces persistent false beliefs—and how environments can be better designed to help people get out of them!

I'm also interested in developing new ways to conduct behavioral research online, and am an active developer of the psiTurk platform [github repository].
